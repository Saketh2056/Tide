# Product

## Register

product

## Users

People who feel they spend too much time on their phone and want a calmer relationship with it. They reach for Tide in charged, low-attention moments — first thing in the morning, last thing at night, or the instant they catch themselves doom-scrolling. The mindset is often restless, tired, or mid-impulse: trying to resist a pull, not study a report. They are *not* quantified-self power users hunting for dashboards; they want gentle help and reassurance. Everything runs on-device with no account, no network, and no tracking — so trust and a sense of safety are part of the job, not a footnote.

## Product Purpose

Tide helps people spend less time on distracting apps and more time on what matters, through **gentle friction rather than hard locks**. The core jobs:

- **Shields** — set a daily time budget or a full block on a specific app.
- **Schedules** — protect recurring hours (a nightly wind-down, a deep-work morning).
- **Focus** — enter distraction-free time with a calm countdown and a deliberate hold-to-end.
- **The Mindful Pause** — the heart of the product: a full-screen, breath-paced moment that opens between impulse and action whenever a blocked app is launched, with an "open anyway" escape hatch that only appears after a slow breath.
- **Today / Insights** — an honest, encouraging picture of screen time, pickups, focus, and streaks.

Success = the user trusts Tide, returns without dread, and genuinely reduces compulsive use *while feeling supported, not policed*.

## Brand Personality

Calm, meditative, and quietly premium. The voice is warm, plain-spoken, and humane — a steady companion, never a scold or a productivity drill-sergeant. Three words: **serene, grounded, trustworthy**. The interface must feel like the antidote to the attention-economy apps it guards against: unhurried, spacious, reassuring. The emotional target is *relief and self-respect*, never guilt, urgency, or gamified pressure.

## Anti-references

- **Neon glassmorphism** — dark mode with glowing violet/mint gradients and frosted-glass cards (the generic "AI wellness app," and Tide's own previous skin). Explicitly rejected.
- **Meditation purple** — Headspace/Calm-style purple-to-blue gradients, soft blobs, and rounded mascots; the saturated cliché of the category.
- **Busy quantified-self dashboard** — dense charts, chips, and numbers competing for attention. Data without calm.
- **Corporate / sterile Material** — cold flat-gray enterprise UI, soulless and generic.
- **Gamified streak-pressure & dark patterns** — anxiety-as-engagement, guilt nudges, manipulative urgency. The product fights these; it must not employ them.

## Design Principles

1. **Practice what you preach.** The app that fights for your attention must never grab it — no dopamine bait, no urgency, no noise. Calm is the product, not a coat of paint.
2. **Friction, not force.** Guide with a gentle pause and a real choice; never humiliate or hard-trap the user. Restraint is a form of kindness.
3. **One calm subject per screen.** Each screen has a single clear focus and a generous amount of nothing around it. Earned familiarity over cleverness.
4. **Honest, humane data.** Show progress plainly and encouragingly; never weaponize numbers or streaks into anxiety.
5. **Quiet confidence.** Premium comes from precision, spacing, and typography — not decoration. Delight is reserved for a few crafted moments (the pause, focus, finishing a session), not sprayed across every pixel.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No special mandate from the owner; hold to sensible, native-grade defaults rather than over-constraining the design. That means readable contrast for primary content, comfortable touch targets, and respecting the system's reduced-motion preference in the breathing and celebration moments. Light, Dark, and System themes are all first-class and must each feel deliberately crafted.
